## Team Reflection

### What Went Well
- Clear division of responsibilities - each person had distinct areas to focus on
- Good communication through group chat and regular meetings
- Everyone completed their assigned tasks on time
- Effective code review process - caught bugs before submission
- Strong collaboration between database and Java development teams

### Challenges Faced
- Coordinating schedules for team meetings was difficult
- Understanding transactional workflow took multiple iterations
- Debugging SQL stored procedures required extra research
- Video recording and editing took longer than anticipated
- Some merge conflicts in shared files that needed resolution

### Lessons Learned
- Starting early and breaking work into smaller tasks helps avoid last-minute stress
- Testing incrementally saves time compared to testing everything at the end
- Clear documentation makes it easier for teammates to understand each other's work
- Version control (Git) is essential for collaborative coding projects
- Asking for help early prevents bigger problems later

### Overall Teamwork Assessment
Our team worked well together throughout the semester. We divided work based on each person's strengths - Sebastian focused on database design, Sumit on queries and schema, Sarthak on Java development, and Justin on documentation. We had regular check-ins to ensure everyone was on track and helped each other when someone got stuck. The most successful aspect was our communication - we responded quickly to questions and shared progress updates regularly. While we faced some challenges with scheduling and debugging, we worked through them collaboratively. Overall, this project taught us valuable lessons about teamwork, time management, and technical skills that will be useful in future projects.
